Welcome

This website was created to support our passionate, hardworking student-athletes as they compete in the exciting world of Ultimate Frisbee. As a student-run project, our goal is to raise donations and generate revenue to fund travel, equipment, and tournament fees—ensuring our team can compete at its best.

How do we do it? Each year, we host an Ultimate Frisbee Combine, where players showcase their speed, agility, throwing accuracy, and endurance. Their performance stats are tracked, analyzed, and proudly displayed here. Whether you're a fan, scout, fellow player, or just curious about the sport, this site lets you follow our athletes' progress and see their dedication firsthand.

By visiting, sharing, or contributing, you’re helping grow the next generation of Ultimate players. Explore our combine results, meet the team, and consider donating to keep our spirit flying high. Thanks for your support—let’s make every throw count!

From Nitro to Steel

From Nitro to Steel: How Portland's AUDL Team Reinvented Its Identity

In 2024, Portland's semi-professional Ultimate Frisbee team underwent a striking transformation, rebranding from the Portland Nitro to the Portland Steel—a change that reflects both the city's industrial soul and the team's evolving identity. This shift isn't just a name swap; it's a statement about resilience, community, and the gritty spirit of Portland itself.

The Meaning Behind the Names

  • Nitro (2021–2023): The original name captured explosive energy, speed, and the high-octane style of Ultimate. It mirrored the team's fast breaks and flashy plays, embodying the adrenaline of the sport.
  • Steel (2024–present): The new identity pays homage to Portland's industrial roots—from its historic steel bridges to its blue-collar work ethic. Steel represents strength, durability, and adaptability, qualities that define both the team and the city.

A Nod to Portland's Culture

Portland is a city of makers, artists, and fighters. The Steel Bridge, a century-old icon, symbolizes connection and endurance—just like Ultimate, which thrives on teamwork and perseverance. The rebrand embraces this legacy, aligning the team with the unyielding spirit of Portlanders who weather storms (literal and metaphorical) together.

What It Means for the Team

The Steel aren't just changing jerseys; they're doubling down on community and grit. The name reflects a shift from "flash" to "foundation," prioritizing:

  • Long-term growth in Portland's Ultimate scene
  • Youth engagement, inspiring the next generation of players
  • Resilience, both on the field (grinding out wins) and off (building a lasting franchise)

A Fresh Chapter

While Nitro was about sparking excitement, Steel is about enduring impact. As the team forges ahead, it carries Portland's identity with it—strong, unbreakable, and united.

Lewis & Clark Pioneers Make History with 2024 DIII Ultimate National Championship

In a stunning display of skill and determination, the Lewis & Clark College Pioneers claimed their first-ever NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIP at the 2024 DIII College Ultimate Championships in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. The Portland-based team capped off an undefeated season with a dominant performance that has electrified the Northwest Ultimate community.

The Road to Glory

Championship by the Numbers:
• 15-0 perfect season record
• +187 point differential in Nationals
• 3 All-Tournament Team selections
• First Northwest team to win DIII title since 2017

The Pioneers' journey to the championship featured a 15-2 victory over Middlebury in semifinals followed by a hard-fought 13-9 win against Colorado College in the finals. Their trademark vertical stack offense and lockdown zone defense proved unstoppable throughout the tournament.

Portland's Ultimate Ascendancy

This championship cements Portland as a growing powerhouse in collegiate Ultimate. With the Portland Steel (AUDL) representing the city at the professional level and now Lewis & Clark's national title, the Rose City's Ultimate scene is reaching new heights.

"This win isn't just for our program - it's for every young player in Portland who dreams of competing at the highest level," said captain Emma Rodriguez. "We hope this inspires more local talent to pursue Ultimate."

What's Next for the Pioneers?

With most of their roster returning next season, Lewis & Clark is poised to defend their title in 2025. The team plans to:

  • Host youth clinics in Portland this summer
  • Expand their recruiting pipeline across the Northwest
  • Challenge DI programs in preseason tournaments

The Pioneers' championship banner will be raised this fall at their home field in Portland, serving as inspiration for the next generation of Northwest Ultimate players.
